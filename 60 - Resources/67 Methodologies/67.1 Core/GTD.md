# GTD

> Get everything out of your head into a trusted system, then decide the next action — so your mind is free to think, not to remember.

## How it works
Five steps form a loop:

1. **Capture** everything into an inbox (`10 - Inbox`).
2. **Clarify** each item: is it actionable?
3. **Organize** it — next action, project, someday/maybe, or reference (`90 - GTD`).
4. **Reflect** in a weekly review.
5. **Engage** — do the next action.

GTD answers *"what do I do next?"*; PARA answers *"where does this live?"*.

## Source
David Allen, *Getting Things Done* (2001).

## Bridge
- **GTD Capture** is the front door of **CODE Capture**; the inbox feeds fleeting notes. See [[CeReBro (CODE)]], [[Zettelkasten]].
- **PARA** receives clarified items and files them by actionability. See [[PARA]].
- The weekly **Reflect** step pairs naturally with [[Spaced Repetition]] review of permanent notes.

## Worked examples
- **Example 1 — Weekly review.** During the review you empty `10 - Inbox`: actionable items become next-actions in `90 - GTD/91 Tasks`; non-actionable-but-maybe items go to `93 Someday-Maybe`; references move to `60 - Resources`.
- **Example 2 — A waiting-for item.** You delegate a task and log it in `90 - GTD/94 Waiting-For` with a follow-up date, so it resurfaces at the next review instead of being forgotten.
