# Evergreen Notes

> Notes that are **atomic, concept-oriented, densely linked, and written to be revisited** — developed continuously over time rather than written once and filed.

## How it works
Andy Matuschak's principles refine the permanent-note idea:

- **Atomic** — one concept per note.
- **Concept-oriented** — titled by the idea, not the source or date.
- **Densely linked** — each note connects outward; the link graph is the structure.
- **Written for your future self** — full sentences, claims you can build on.

## Source
Andy Matuschak, "Evergreen notes" (notes.andymatuschak.org, 2020).

## Bridge
A modern articulation of [[Zettelkasten]] permanent notes; the "densely linked, concept-oriented" discipline is exactly what makes notes good **context units** for the [[LLM Wiki]]. Opt in when you want stricter note-quality standards than baseline Zettelkasten.

## Worked examples
- **Example 1 — Concept-titled note.** Instead of "Notes from Ahrens ch.3" you write a note titled "Writing is thinking, not recording it," which can be linked from many contexts.
- **Example 2 — Continuous development.** A note is edited across months as your understanding deepens; its address and title stay stable so inbound links never break.
