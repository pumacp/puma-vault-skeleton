# SQ3R

> A five-step reading method — **Survey, Question, Read, Recite, Review** — for actively understanding non-academic texts.

## How it works
1. **Survey** — skim headings, summaries, and structure first.
2. **Question** — turn each heading into a question to answer.
3. **Read** — read a section seeking the answers.
4. **Recite** — answer from memory in your own words.
5. **Review** — consolidate across the whole text.

## Source
Francis P. Robinson, *Effective Study* (1946).

## Bridge
A general-purpose reading protocol that parallels [[Zettelkasten]]'s literature-note step — the "Recite" output becomes a literature note. Where Keshav's Three-Pass suits academic papers, SQ3R suits books and articles for the **Student** and **Writer** profiles.

## Worked examples
- **Example 1 — A non-fiction book.** You survey the table of contents, convert each chapter title into a question, and capture one literature note per chapter answering it.
- **Example 2 — A long article.** The "Recite" step forces you to write the takeaway in your own words, which is exactly the raw material for a permanent note.
