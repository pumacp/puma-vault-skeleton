# Feynman Technique

> Understand something by explaining it simply, as if teaching a beginner — the gaps where you stumble show you what you do not yet understand.

## How it works
1. Pick a concept and write an explanation in plain language.
2. Pretend to teach it to someone with no background.
3. Note where you hand-wave or fall back on jargon — those are your gaps.
4. Return to the source, then re-explain until it is simple and complete.

## Source
Associated with physicist Richard Feynman; popularized via James Gleick, *Genius* (1992) and study-skills literature.

## Bridge
A distill-by-teaching test that feeds [[Zettelkasten]] permanent notes: a concept you can explain simply is ready to be written as an atomic note in your own words. Strong fit for the **Teacher** profile and the **Express** phase of [[CeReBro (CODE)]].

## Worked examples
- **Example 1 — Explaining "attention".** Trying to explain the attention mechanism without equations reveals you cannot yet say *why* it helps — sending you back to the source before you write the note.
- **Example 2 — Teaching to test mastery.** Before an exam, a student explains each topic aloud; the topics that break down become the night's study list.
