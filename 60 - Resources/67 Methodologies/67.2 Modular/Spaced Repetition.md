# Spaced Repetition

> Review material at increasing intervals, just before you would forget it — the most efficient way to move knowledge into long-term memory.

## How it works
- Active recall: test yourself rather than reread.
- Spacing: each successful recall lengthens the interval to the next review.
- Tooling: Obsidian's spaced-repetition plugin (or Anki) schedules reviews over your own notes.

## Source
Spacing effect: Hermann Ebbinghaus (1885). Testing effect: Karpicke & Roediger, *Science* (2008).

## Bridge
A **review layer over [[Zettelkasten]] permanent notes**, and a natural extension of the **Reflect** step in [[GTD]]'s weekly review. Opt in when retention (not just reference) matters — teaching, exams, language learning.

## Worked examples
- **Example 1 — Note-to-card.** A permanent note's key claim is turned into a question/answer card; the plugin resurfaces it on a widening schedule until it sticks.
- **Example 2 — Weekly cadence.** Spaced reviews are folded into the GTD weekly review, so consolidating memory becomes part of an existing habit rather than a new one.
